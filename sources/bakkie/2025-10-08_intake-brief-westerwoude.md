# Background

Gemeente Westerwoude (140k inhabitants, three urban districts plus seven surrounding kernen) handles roughly 38,000 citizen contacts per year on waste-collection topics. About 62% of these come through our Klantcontactcentrum (KCC) by phone, 24% via webformulier, the rest by email or in-person at the loket. Internal benchmarking suggests this is roughly twice the per-capita load of comparable municipalities, which we attribute to our split-system collection (separate inzameling for rest, GFT, PMD, papier, plus the milieustraten for grofvuil) being more complex than the national norm.

Top topics by volume, based on KCC's 2024 categorisation:

1. Pickup schedule questions ("wanneer wordt mijn PMD opgehaald?") — ~31%
2. Grofvuil pickup booking — ~18%
3. Bin requests (new / replacement / extra) — ~14%
4. Complaints about overflowing containers, dumping, missed pickups — ~12%
5. Questions about milieustraten openingstijden and what's accepted — ~9%
6. Everything else (verhuizing, large-household tariff, tuinafval, etc.) — ~16%

The college has committed to digital-first service delivery as part of the coalition agreement (2024-2028). The waste domain is the natural starting point given volume and citizen frustration scores in our 2024 burgerpeiling.

# What we'd like

A conversational assistant ("chatbot," for short) on the gemeente website that:

- Answers the top topics above accurately and in plain Dutch
- Is able to act, not just inform — e.g. actually books a grofvuil pickup, actually files a melding openbare ruimte, actually requests a bin
- Is available 24/7 and substantially reduces the volume reaching KCC
- Hands off to a human agent gracefully when needed
- Logs interactions for quality monitoring (AVG-compliant)
- Integrates with the existing systems: AfvalKern (collection scheduling and bin registration, supplied by OmgevingsSoft BV), MOR (meldingen openbare ruimte), and our generic CMS

We would also be interested in exploring, in a later phase, expansion to other domains: parking permits, paspoort/rijbewijs appointments, perhaps participation processes. But the eerste fase should focus on waste.

# Constraints we know about

- AVG / DPIA required before live launch. Marja Lievens (our FG) will be involved from the start.
- Existing AfvalKern integration is contractually exclusive to OmgevingsSoft for read/write. They will need to deliver API endpoints we don't currently have. We have a productive relationship with them but haven't yet discussed this scope.
- Budget envelope: indicatively €280k–€340k for fase 1 (build + pilot), pending college approval in Q1 2026.
- Coalition partners have asked for a visible service win before the next gemeenteraadsverkiezingen (March 2026). Realistically the pilot won't land that quickly, but we'd like communicable progress by then.

# What we're hoping KAI brings

We have looked at three off-the-shelf vendors and one in-house build option with our internal team. None feel quite right. We want a partner who can think with us about the right model, build a custom assistant that actually works in Dutch and on our specific topics, and be honest about what's not yet possible.
